# Industry / Competitive Expert — Group Digest (audit-2026-06)

## Executive summary

Since the prior competitive audits, MeetingScribe has quietly shipped the category's hardest-to-build features: local RAG grounding with cited deep-links (`ChatSession.groundedSystemPrompt` + `searchVaultHybrid`), on-device semantic embeddings (`EmbeddingService` + `vault_embeddings`), speaker-labeled diarized transcripts, a cross-meeting `DecisionStore` ledger, "On this day" resurfacing, and — crucially — an Obsidian-native vault (YAML frontmatter, `[[wikilinks]]`, `#tags`, `DailyNoteWriter`). On the local-first / "second brain you own" axis it is now ahead of Granola, Otter, Dex, and Limitless, exactly as the 2026 market (Meta's Limitless shutdown, Amazon's Bee buy, the Recall backlash, NYC Bar consent rulings, Pieces dropping Ollama) made on-device trust the decisive moat.

The remaining gaps are **not capability gaps but surfacing, breadth, and platform gaps**. Five themes recur across all five agents:

1. **The half-built RAG must finish** — grounding is meeting-summary-only (`prefix(1200)`, top-5, `entityKind == "meeting"`); it drops transcripts, voice notes, encounters, people, daily notes, and speaker attribution.
2. **The MCP server is frozen on the Nov-2024 spec** — `protocolVersion 2024-11-05`, tools-only, stdio-only, 23–24 write/lookup tools but **zero recall/search tools, no resources, no prompts**. The app's richest moat (relationship graph + encounters + coaching) is under-exposed, and coaching frameworks only cover 2 of 7 relationship types.
3. **The macOS 26 Tahoe platform is massively under-exploited** — Apple Foundation Models (free on-device LLM) is unused while the app forces an Ollama install; App Intents are a stub; no WidgetKit/Control Center, no CoreSpotlight, no EventKit/Reminders write-back.
4. **In-meeting + proactive layers are missing** — no scratchpad, no mid-call recap, no calendar auto-record, no proactive pre-meeting brief; the whole category now competes here.
5. **Privacy and retention are invisible** — the strongest claim ("0 bytes leave this Mac") is stated nowhere, and the vault grows unbounded with no forget/TTL primitive (and orphaned embedding vectors on delete).

The net-new bets below convert the local-first/no-bot moat into capabilities the cloud field structurally cannot match.

## Top 5 bets

> **1. Finish the RAG loop (whole-vault grounding + speaker attribution + scope chips + citations).** The single highest-value, half-built feature. It converts "meeting search" into a real second brain that no cloud competitor can match because it runs 100% on-device. (#1)
>
> **2. Modernize + extend the MCP server: 2025-06-18 spec, a `search_everything` tool, and a session-start relationship-brief resource — and fix the coaching frameworks first.** The relationship graph + encounters + coaching is the app's one undefended moat; today it's frozen on a 2024 spec, has zero recall tools, and gives generic platitudes for 5 of 7 relationship types. (#2, #5, #7, #4)
>
> **3. Apple Foundation Models as a zero-install LLM backend behind an `LLMProvider` protocol.** Removes the #1 onboarding cliff (install Ollama + pull a multi-GB model) using a Tahoe framework that didn't exist when prior plans were written — while keeping "nothing leaves your device." (#8)
>
> **4. Become a native macOS verb: finish the App Intent suite, EventKit/Reminders write-back, CoreSpotlight, and a real WidgetKit/Control Center surface.** The local-first app is invisible to Spotlight Actions, Reminders, the desktop, and Control Center — closing these turns "private notetaker" into "the Mac's native AI memory layer." (#10, #11, #21, #23)
>
> **5. Make the privacy moat provable and the data ownership explicit.** A live-values privacy panel + consent helper + MCP privacy line (S effort, high leverage), plus leaning into "your vault IS an Obsidian vault" — the data-ownership story Granola/Otter/Fathom structurally cannot tell. (#3, #17)
