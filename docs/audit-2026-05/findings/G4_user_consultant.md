# End-user — Independent Consultant / Freelancer

*Lens: I serve 6–8 clients at once. I need notes siloed by client, client-ready summaries I can send without leaking other clients' data, scope/commitment tracking per engagement, and billable hours I can pull straight off recorded calls.*

## Full-app audit (through my lens)

I walked my real workflow against the live source: tag a call by client → make a clean summary to send the client → track scope/decisions/commitments per engagement → bill the time → keep client data siloed. Verdict: the primitives exist for a *single* knowledge base, but nothing in the model knows that "Acme" and "Globex" are separate businesses with separate confidentiality boundaries. There is no client/workspace concept anywhere.

**Tagging a meeting by client works — but it's flat, single-namespace metadata, not a silo.** `MeetingTag` (`Models/Tag.swift:17-66`) is a bag of id/name/symbol/color with optional kind/date/location. `TagStore.primaryTag(for:)` is literally just `tags(for: meeting).first` (`Storage/TagStore.swift:144-146`) — order-dependent, no notion of "this tag is the owning client." Presets are Tyler's internal teams ("Skio", "Recharge", "Product Team Calls" — `Tag.swift:58-65`), i.e. built for an employee, not a consultant with an external client roster. There's nothing stopping me tagging one call "Acme" and "Internal" and having it silently land under whichever sorts first.

**Client data is NOT siloed on disk — and a recent fix made that worse for me.** `MeetingStore.desiredDirectory` (`Storage/MeetingStore.swift:101-110`) now ignores the tag entirely and writes every meeting to a date-partitioned path `meetings/yyyy/yyyy-MM/<slug>`. The comment is explicit: "tag is metadata, not a path component" (`:94-100`). That was the ENG-B fix — good for migration sanity, but it means I can no longer hand a client (or a subpoena, or my own backup script) the *folder* of just their calls. Everything is interleaved by date. The Obsidian frontmatter still derives a tag from the folder name one level up (`Export/ObsidianExporter.swift:36-37`) — which under the new date layout will read `yyyy-MM`, not the client. So even the exported `tags:` field is now wrong for grouped consumption.

**People CRM is single-org-shaped.** `VaultKit/Person.swift` has `company: String?` (one string) and free-form `tags: [String]`. There is no Organization/Client entity, no "this contact belongs to Acme," no way to see "everyone I know at Globex" except by string-matching a free-text company field that import sources spell three different ways. For someone whose entire business is *which contact at which client*, the data model is missing the middle tier.

**Export is a raw full dump — unsafe to send a client.** `MeetingExporter.combinedMarkdown` (`Export/MeetingExporter.swift:14-38`) concatenates Summary + My Notes + **full Transcript** into one markdown/PDF. My private notes ("client is unhappy, may churn, pad the estimate") and the verbatim transcript go out with the summary. There is no redaction, no "summary-only" mode, no recipient, no branding/letterhead. PDF rendering is a hardcoded US-Letter `NSTextView` print (`:56-91`) — no logo, no header/footer, no "Prepared by [me] for [client]". The Obsidian export embeds attendees as `[[wikilinks]]` and inline `#hashtags` (`ObsidianExporter.swift:95-144`) — great for *my* vault, actively wrong to send a client (it leaks my tagging taxonomy and links).

**No billable-time anything.** `Meeting` (`Models/Meeting.swift`) has `startDate`/`endDate` so duration is derivable, but there is no rate, no billable flag, no rounding, no per-client rate card, no timesheet, no invoice. The richest "grouping above a meeting" is `ActionItems/Project.swift` (Notion-style page with `meetingIDs` + `initiativeID`) — a genuine near-miss engagement entity, but it lives inside the task board, isn't client-scoped, and carries no commercial fields.

**No engagement/project entity above meetings that a consultant would recognize.** `WorkspaceLinks.swift` cross-links entities via `meetingscribe://` URLs, and `Project` can link meetings — but there's no first-class "Engagement" with a client, a SOW, a budget, a scope baseline, and a status. Scope-change and decision history are buried in free-text summaries with no structure to diff.

## Existing-plan items I rank highest

1. **Per-tag summary templates** (V3 §4 / REMAINING_WORK §4). For me this is per-*client* templates — a client who wants "Decisions + Next Steps only" vs one who wants a narrative recap. Highest-leverage existing idea for my workflow; today `NoteTemplate.all` is generic and not tag-scoped.
2. **Send the follow-up in Mail with attendees prefilled** (V3 §4, marked done). This is how I deliver recaps. Critical that it pulls *client* attendees, not my whole roster.
3. **Full-width + remove width caps (LAY-1)** — my decision/commitment logs are tables (date · decision · owner · billable?). The 720pt prose cap (`NotionDesign.contentMaxWidth`) crushes them.
4. **Unified "find everything about X" (searchAll → GlobalSearchView)** — scoped to a client, this is "show me every call, commitment, and contact for Acme." Indispensable before a renewal conversation.
5. **De-hardcode the user name** (done) — matters because my exports/follow-ups represent *me, the vendor*, to a client; a baked-in "Tyler" in prompts would leak into client-facing text.

## NET-NEW recommendations

**U4-1 — First-class `Client`/`Workspace` entity above tags.** *What:* a real `Client { id, name, brandColor, logo, confidentialityLevel, defaultRate }` that owns meetings (via primary-client field on `Meeting`), people (via `Person.clientID`), engagements, and tasks. Tags stay for sub-categorization within a client. *Why:* every other gap below depends on the app knowing client boundaries; today it doesn't. *Value:* the foundational silo. *Effort:* L. *Impact:* High. *Depends on:* nothing (foundation).

**U4-2 — Per-client confidentiality wall (siloed view + export guard).** *What:* a client-scoped mode that filters Today/Meetings/People/Search to one client, and an export-time guard that refuses to include another client's data in a shared artifact. Restore optional client-grouped on-disk layout (`Clients/<name>/meetings/…`) as an export/mirror target, since `desiredDirectory` no longer groups. *Why:* I'm contractually obligated to keep clients' data separate; right now one search box surfaces all of them. *Value:* compliance + the ability to hand over "just your folder." *Effort:* M. *Impact:* High. *Depends on:* U4-1.

**U4-3 — Redacted, client-branded shareable summary.** *What:* an "Export for client" path distinct from `MeetingExporter.combinedMarkdown` that (a) excludes `notes.md` (my private notes) and optionally the transcript, (b) strips `#tags`/`[[wikilinks]]`, (c) applies client logo + "Prepared by {me} for {client}" letterhead to the PDF, (d) previews exactly what leaves. *Why:* the current export dumps my private notes and the verbatim transcript — unsendable. *Value:* I can send a recap in one click without manual scrubbing. *Effort:* M. *Impact:* High. *Depends on:* U4-1 (for branding), else standalone.

**U4-4 — Billable-time capture from recordings.** *What:* mark a meeting billable with a per-client/per-engagement rate (rate card on `Client`), auto-fill duration from `endDate−startDate`, support rounding rules (e.g. 6-min increments), and a "non-billable" flag. *Why:* I already record the call; the billable minutes are sitting in the timestamps unused. *Value:* eliminates a separate timesheet. *Effort:* M. *Impact:* High. *Depends on:* U4-1.

**U4-5 — Per-client timesheet + invoice-ready export.** *What:* a per-client dashboard rolling up billable meetings into a date-range timesheet (CSV/PDF) with totals and amounts; "export for invoicing." *Why:* month-end I need hours-by-client without re-deriving from the calendar. *Value:* turns recorded calls directly into an invoice line-item table. *Effort:* M. *Impact:* High. *Depends on:* U4-4.

**U4-6 — Engagement entity (SOW-aware) above meetings.** *What:* promote `Project` into a client-scoped `Engagement { clientID, scopeBaseline, budget, status, startDate, endDate }` that owns meetings, tasks, and a structured scope/decision log. *Why:* `Project` is the near-miss (`Project.swift` has `meetingIDs`/`initiativeID`) but isn't client-scoped or commercial. *Value:* a home for everything about one piece of work. *Effort:* L. *Impact:* High. *Depends on:* U4-1.

**U4-7 — Scope-change detection from summaries.** *What:* when a new meeting summary mentions work outside the engagement's `scopeBaseline`, flag a "possible scope change" with the quote, and let me convert it to a logged change-order line. *Why:* unbilled scope creep is how consultants lose money; the local LLM already summarizes every call. *Value:* catches "can you also just…" asks before they become free work. *Effort:* M. *Impact:* High. *Depends on:* U4-6.

**U4-8 — Structured commitment/decision ledger per engagement.** *What:* extract "X committed to Y by Z" and "we decided A" into a typed ledger (who/what/when/status), distinct from action items, queryable across an engagement's calls. *Why:* today decisions live as free text in summaries; I can't diff or audit them. *Value:* "what did we agree to in March?" answered instantly — and defensibly. *Effort:* M. *Impact:* Med. *Depends on:* U4-6 (links best to engagement; works standalone).

**U4-9 — Client-aware follow-up recipients + safe attendee scoping.** *What:* when drafting a follow-up (`FollowUpGeneratorService`), prefill *only* that client's attendees and never auto-CC contacts from other clients; warn if an attendee belongs to a different client. *Why:* the planned "send in Mail" prefill must not mix client rosters. *Value:* prevents the catastrophic wrong-client-CC. *Effort:* S. *Impact:* High. *Depends on:* U4-1.

**U4-10 — Organization grouping in People (contacts by client).** *What:* give `Person` a `clientID` (or normalize `company`) and a People view grouped by client/org, so "everyone at Globex" is one click. *Why:* `Person.company` is a single free-text string with no org entity. *Value:* my CRM matches how I think — by account. *Effort:* M. *Impact:* Med. *Depends on:* U4-1.

**U4-11 — Per-tag/client default template binding.** *What:* let each `Client` (or tag) bind a default `NoteTemplate` and a default summary style, applied automatically on record. *Why:* extends the planned per-tag templates to auto-apply by client. *Value:* each client's calls come out in the format that client expects, zero setup per call. *Effort:* S. *Impact:* Med. *Depends on:* per-tag templates (planned), U4-1.

**U4-12 — Portable per-client archive export.** *What:* "Export everything for {client}" → a zip of that client's meetings (md + audio + transcripts), engagements, and contact list, with a manifest. *Why:* offboarding a client, or a data-portability/right-to-deletion request, must be one action — and the date-partitioned layout (`MeetingStore.swift:101`) makes manual gathering painful. *Value:* clean handoff/deletion; reinforces the "private/portable" promise. *Effort:* M. *Impact:* Med. *Depends on:* U4-1, U4-2.

## Top 3 picks

1. **U4-1 — First-class Client/Workspace entity.** Without it, "does it support multiple clients cleanly?" is *no*. Tags are flat metadata and `desiredDirectory` deliberately ignores them, so there is no boundary the app can enforce. Everything else I want hangs off this.
2. **U4-3 — Redacted, client-branded shareable summary.** The single most dangerous current behavior: `combinedMarkdown` sends my private notes + full transcript to clients. A safe, branded "Export for client" is the feature I'd use on every single call.
3. **U4-4 + U4-5 — Billable-time capture and per-client timesheet.** The timestamps to bill from already exist on `Meeting`; surfacing them as billable hours and an invoice-ready per-client rollup turns the app from a note-taker into a revenue tool for freelancers.
