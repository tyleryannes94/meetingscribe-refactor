# Product Management — Roadmap, Prioritization & Requirement Gaps

> Lens: Is the four-phase plan investing in the right outcomes, in the right order, to turn a working *personal app* into a *product* — and what jobs-to-be-done do the plans never name?

## Full-app audit (through my lens)

**The plans are healthy on execution and thin on outcomes.** V2 (`MASTER_PLAN_V2.md`) is an architecture/data-layer plan; V3 (`MASTER_PLAN_V3.md`) is a UX-fix-list plan. Both are organized by *severity of defect* (P0/P1/P2), not by *user outcome*. That is correct for a refinement phase, but it means the roadmap optimizes for "finish the rebuild correctly" and never asks "what is the one job this app must nail." The git log (`c25a41e`…`0c0ca79`) shows the team is executing the V3 list fast and faithfully — up-next hero, needs-attention, stay-in-touch nudges, ⌘N, inline person edit, follow-up-in-Mail, write-capable MCP, SHA-256 model pin are all already landed. So the *execution risk is low*; the *strategy risk is that the backlog is almost entirely table-stakes parity work*, with the genuine differentiators ("best-in-class ideas") parked in V3 §4 as optional and GUI-heavy.

**The single most important JTBD is under-served by the roadmap.** Walking the code, the product has three latent jobs competing for investment: (1) *capture* a meeting losslessly, (2) *recall* what happened/was decided/owed, (3) *maintain relationships* via the People graph. V2 §"Competitive Position" correctly names the People graph as the moat — but the roadmap spends Phases 0–2 almost entirely on (1) capture-correctness and architecture (transcript truncation, vault migration, two-binary split, NSFileCoordinator). The *recall* job — the thing a user actually opens the app to do the morning after a meeting — has no dedicated phase. The summarizer is a single hardcoded template (`OllamaService.swift:268-306`); the pre-meeting brief is a *static list* of prior meetings + open items with **no synthesis** (`PreMeetingBriefView.swift:19-92`, no Ollama call in the brief path); there is no cross-meeting or series-level rollup even though `seriesID` exists (`MeetingCard.swift:109`, `MeetingDetailHeader.swift:60`). The app transcribes beautifully and then makes you re-read transcripts to recall anything.

**The "personal app → product" gap is real and the plans don't bridge it.** Three structural tells:
- **Zero instrumentation.** V2 defines four KPIs (cold launch <1s, record latency <200ms, transcription lag, crash rate) and a target table, but nothing in `Sources/` measures them — `grep -ril "analytics\|telemetry"` returns only incidental matches (`AppError.swift`, `Initiative.swift`). You cannot manage a roadmap to targets you don't measure. For a local-first/privacy app this must be *local-only* metrics, but their total absence means every "is this better?" decision is vibes.
- **No activation/retention loop is designed.** Onboarding exists (`OnboardingSheet.swift`) but the roadmap's success criterion is "stable monolith / iCloud-native vault," not "user records meeting #2." There is no aha-moment definition, no first-week journey.
- **The product can't be given to a second person.** Everything assumes one user ("Me"/"Them" baked into the prompt, single profile name). That's fine for a personal tool but the audit asks us to think product — and nothing in the four phases moves an inch toward shareable output, multi-identity, or a "send this to a colleague" surface (Team files were *deleted*, correctly, but nothing replaces the underlying job of "get this meeting's outcome to someone who wasn't there").

**Sequencing risk: Phase 2 (two-binary split) is the highest-cost, lowest-user-visible-value item and it sits ahead of recall/intelligence work.** `REMAINING_WORK.md` §2 lays out a 5-step daemon activation that is multi-week, Mac-only, untestable in CI, and carries an explicit ENG-A regression risk (threading dropped-chunk counts across XPC). Its entire payoff is "a Whisper crash won't kill the meeting list." That's real reliability value, but the audit report (`AUDIT_REPORT_2026-05-30.md` §2) shows the monolith is already stable and all six Phase-0 concurrency bugs are fixed. Doing the daemon split *before* shipping any of the recall/synthesis differentiators front-loads the riskiest, least-differentiating work. **Recommendation: demote two-binary activation behind the recall bets below.**

**Over-investment:** capture-path correctness now has diminishing returns (ENG-A/B/C/D/F all landed; only the ENG-A coverage-gate residual remains — `MeetingPipelineController.swift:88`). **Under-investment:** anything that turns the captured corpus into ongoing leverage — synthesis across meetings, decision/commitment tracking, periodic review, search as a first-class surface (the chat already has 30+ read/write tools across meetings/people/tasks/files — `Chat/*.swift` — but there is no non-chat "ask my vault" entry for users who won't type into a rail).

## Existing-plan items I rank highest (through my lens)

1. **Write-capable MCP** (V3 §4 — already shipped, `6cdec9c`). Highest strategic ROI in the whole plan: it makes the vault *actionable by an agent*, which is the only durable moat vs. Granola/Otter. Endorse continued investment here over UI polish.
2. **Unified "find everything about X" → wire `searchAll()` into `GlobalSearchView`** (V3 §4 / REMAINING_WORK §4). Directly serves the *recall* JTBD; the FTS5 v2 schema + recency boost already exist (`SecondBrainDB.swift`), so this is high-value/low-cost. The view shell exists (`GlobalSearchView.swift`) — finish it.
3. **Per-tag summary templates** (V3 §4). Endorse and *upgrade to P1*: the single fixed prompt is the biggest quality ceiling on the recall job. A 1:1 vs all-hands vs interview vs decision-review template materially changes output usefulness.
4. **Speaker-labeled transcript & summary** (V3 §4). `SpeakerDiarization.swift` exists unsurfaced; attributing decisions/asks to people is what makes the People graph defensible. Endorse, but sequence *after* templates.
5. **ENG-A coverage-gate residual** (AUDIT §1). The one remaining data-loss path; cheap; protects trust. Do it, but it's the *last* capture-correctness item — then stop investing there.

## NET-NEW recommendations

### P1-1 — Decision & Commitment Ledger (cross-meeting "what was decided / who owes what")
**What:** A first-class `Decision` entity (distinct from `ActionItem`), extracted from the existing "Key Decisions" summary section, with a dedicated surface that shows *open decisions, reversals, and unresolved commitments across all meetings* — not per-meeting. **Why:** The summary already produces "Key Decisions" (`OllamaService.swift:286`) but they die inside one meeting's markdown. The recall JTBD is "what did we decide about X and has it changed?" No competitor surfaces a queryable decision history. **User value:** the morning-after and quarterly-review payoff that justifies recording at all. **Effort:** M (extractor + entity + view; reuses FTS5). **Impact:** High. **Depends on:** per-tag templates (P1-3 below) help but not required.

### P1-2 — Series continuity: carry-forward + recurring-meeting rollup
**What:** For meetings sharing a `seriesID`, (a) auto-carry *unresolved* action items into the next occurrence's brief, and (b) generate a series-level rollup ("across your last 6 1:1s with Alice: open threads, recurring topics, sentiment drift"). **Why:** `seriesID` is modeled and shown as a chip (`MeetingCard.swift:109`) but does nothing functional; the PreMeetingBrief is a static list (`PreMeetingBriefView.swift`). Recurring 1:1s/standups are the highest-frequency meeting type and currently get zero memory between instances. **User value:** the app *remembers the thread* so the user doesn't re-prep. **Effort:** M. **Impact:** High. **Depends on:** P1-1 (shared unresolved-commitment query).

### P1-3 — LLM-generated pre-meeting brief (make the brief actually brief *for* you)
**What:** Replace the static brief with a synthesized one: "Last time you discussed X and committed to Y (still open); they raised Z; suggested talking points." **Why:** `PreMeetingBriefView` lists raw prior meetings/open items with no synthesis — the user still has to read everything. The summarization engine already exists; just point it at the prior-meeting set. **User value:** turns 5 min of pre-meeting scramble into a 15-second read. **Effort:** S–M (reuse `OllamaService.generate`). **Impact:** High. **Depends on:** P1-2 for series-aware input.

### P1-4 — Local-only product analytics so the roadmap can be managed to its own KPIs
**What:** A tiny on-device `Metrics` recorder (JSON in App Support, never synced, never networked) capturing the four V2 KPIs + funnel events (onboarding done, meeting #1/#2 recorded, summary viewed, follow-up sent). Surface in a hidden Diagnostics panel. **Why:** V2 sets KPI targets with *no measurement*; `Sources/` has no telemetry. You cannot prioritize the next phase without knowing where users actually drop. **User value:** indirect (better roadmap), plus a "your stats" view is a retention hook. **Effort:** S. **Impact:** Med (High for decision quality). **Depends on:** none.

### P1-5 — Weekly Review surface (the periodic recall ritual)
**What:** A "This Week" view/digest: meetings recorded, decisions made, commitments closed vs. opened, people you met, follow-ups still pending — generated locally, exportable. **Why:** The plan has an *end-of-day recap* idea (V3 TDY-6, P2) but no weekly horizon, which is the cadence at which knowledge workers actually review. Pairs with P1-1/P1-4 (decisions + metrics already structured). **User value:** the recurring reason to *reopen* the app, not just record into it — the missing retention loop. **Effort:** M. **Impact:** High. **Depends on:** P1-1 (decisions), P1-4 (event data) ideal but not blocking.

### P1-6 — "Share this outcome" — portable meeting recap export
**What:** One-click export of a meeting's summary + decisions + action items as a clean, recipient-safe artifact (Markdown/PDF/`mailto` body) with private notes stripped. **Why:** The follow-up generator does email *drafting* (`FollowUpView.swift:140`) but the broader job — "get this meeting's outcome to someone who wasn't there" — has no clean shareable surface (Team features were deleted). This is the lowest-cost bridge from "personal app" to "produces things other people consume." **User value:** the recap becomes the deliverable, not just a private note. **Effort:** S–M (MeetingExporter already does Markdown/Drive). **Impact:** Med-High. **Depends on:** none.

### P1-7 — Ask-my-vault command bar (non-chat recall entry point)
**What:** A ⌘K command palette that runs `searchAll()` *and* can answer a natural-language question by invoking the existing chat tool loop headlessly, returning an inline answer card — without making the user open and converse in the chat rail. **Why:** The chat already has 30+ tools (`Chat/PeopleChatTools.swift`, `MeetingChatTools.swift`, etc.) and a tool loop, but the only entry is a conversational rail that V3 wants *closed by default*. Most recall needs are one-shot ("what did Bob commit to?"), not a conversation. **User value:** instant recall without context-switching into chat. **Effort:** M. **Impact:** High. **Depends on:** searchAll wiring (endorsed item #2).

### P1-8 — Auto-tagging / meeting-type classification (unlock the template system)
**What:** Classify each meeting (1:1, standup, interview, sales, all-hands, decision review) at finalize time from attendees + calendar metadata + transcript, store as a tag. **Why:** Per-tag templates (P1-3 endorse) and series rollups are only as good as the tags; today tagging is manual and tags are display-only filters. **User value:** the right summary shape automatically, no manual curation. **Effort:** M. **Impact:** Med. **Depends on:** per-tag templates to consume it.

### P1-9 — Calendar-aware capture nudge → close the "I forgot to record" gap
**What:** When a detected meeting (`Detection/AmbientMeetingDetector.swift`, Zoom/Meet auto-detect) starts and isn't being recorded, fire a single low-friction "Record this?" notification tied to the calendar event. **Why:** The biggest silent failure of *any* meeting recorder is the meeting you never hit record on. Detection + notifications both exist; they aren't joined into a capture-recovery prompt. **User value:** protects the core capture JTBD at the human-behavior layer, not the audio layer. **Effort:** S–M. **Impact:** Med-High. **Depends on:** none.

### P1-10 — Define and instrument an activation milestone ("recorded meeting #2")
**What:** Pick one north-star activation event, instrument it (via P1-4), and build the onboarding/empty-states to drive toward it (e.g., post-first-summary, prompt "set up calendar so we can prep your next meeting"). **Why:** The roadmap has no activation definition; phase deliverables are infrastructure states. This is the cheapest "act like a product" move. **User value:** indirect but compounding. **Effort:** S. **Impact:** Med. **Depends on:** P1-4.

## Top 3 picks

1. **P1-1 — Decision & Commitment Ledger.** The clearest net-new differentiator: it converts the corpus the app already captures into queryable, cross-meeting institutional memory no competitor offers, and it's the concrete form of "protect the moat." Reuses the FTS5 schema and the existing "Key Decisions" extraction.
2. **P1-3 + P1-2 — Synthesized, series-aware pre-meeting brief.** Highest user-felt value per unit effort: the engine and `seriesID` already exist, the brief view is already there but static. This is the single biggest upgrade to the recall JTBD and the daily-open reason.
3. **P1-4 — Local-only analytics.** Small, unglamorous, and the prerequisite for *every* future prioritization call. Without it the roadmap will keep optimizing defect-severity instead of user outcomes.

**Single highest-priority recommendation overall:** Re-anchor the roadmap from *capture-correctness + architecture* to the **recall job-to-be-done** — ship the Decision/Commitment Ledger (P1-1) and the synthesized series-aware brief (P1-2/P1-3) *before* activating the two-binary split. Capture is essentially solved; the unrealized product value (and the moat) is in turning captured meetings into durable, queryable memory.
